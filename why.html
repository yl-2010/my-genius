Why Arc?

What sets Arc apart from Chrome, Edge, Firefox, and other conventional browsers?

Tab Management:

Tab management in Arc is miles ahead of other browsers.

The biggest improvement is the addition of "spaces". "Spaces" are a group of tabs including pinned and non-pinned tabs. You can have different spaces for different classes or uses. For example, I have an individual space for each period I go to. Meanwhile, your favorite tabs on the top stay the same throughout spaces. All of these tabs are all in the same window, making it really easy to switch between each set of tabs for each class. The closest thing to spaces that normal browsers have are separate windows. In other browsers, people might have different windows for different classes. In Arc, all of your tabs for each class can be held in the same window, just in different spaces. Check out the gallery tab for examples of different spaces!

Another useful tab managing feature are folders. Folders are like tab groups in Chrome or Edge. A folder can have many tabs in it, and can expand or collapse. However, you can actually nest folders within each other, unlike tab groups in Chrome and Edge!

However, my favorite tab management feature is called "Tidy Tabs". When you click on it, it rearranges all of your temporary tabs into group based on topic, turning a mess into something readable.

📑

The Little Things:

There are also many little things Arc does to make things simpler. For example, after you join a Zoom meeting, it automatically deletes the tab with the meeting link. Or when you download a file with a long weird name, it uses AI to rename it something readable.
